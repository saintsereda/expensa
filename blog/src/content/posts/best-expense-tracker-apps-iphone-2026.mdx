## Where each app actually wins

**Money Manager** wins on the "I just want to type a number and move on" experience. Five-star average across 17,000 ratings is not an accident — the form is fast, the widget is good, and 37 languages is genuinely useful. The catch is the free tier shows ads, and there's no automation: every coffee is still your job.

**Monefy** is the speed champion for manual entry. One screen, big category buttons, done. If your tracking style is "I'll log it when I open the app at night," Monefy is fine. The Premium pricing ($39.99–$69.99) is steep for what amounts to a cleaner version of the free tier, and there's no AI anywhere in the app.

**Fealthy** is the only app with native **Monobank sync**, which matters a lot if you bank with Monobank in Ukraine and nothing else. The AI categorization is real, the design is modern. The 15 ratings tell you it's brand-new — features are still landing, the spending category is the only auto-classification today, and there's no offline mode.

**Saldo** is the right answer if you live in the US or Canada and want a Mint-style aggregator that pulls from 700+ banks plus your brokerage and crypto wallets. It's the broadest data coverage in this list. Trade-off: your bank credentials sit with a third party, and the bank integration doesn't cover Ukrainian, EU, or most non-US/CA institutions.

**FinEye** is the closest thing to a "do-everything" app — investments, debts, stocks, crypto, multi-currency, AI categorization, offline mode, family sharing — without bank-account aggregation. CSV/Excel imports stand in for the bank connection. The lifetime price ($79.99) is fair if it sticks. 320 ratings means it's young but past the rough edges.

**Expensa** is the only app in this list that captures **Apple Pay taps automatically**, with no bank login and no aggregator. Every tap-to-pay charge lands in your tracker the second it clears, via a one-time iOS Shortcuts setup. Add AI receipt scanning for the non–Apple Pay leftovers, AI budget allocation that suggests how to split your money based on your actual spending, and shared spaces on iCloud, and you have something the other five apps don't try to do — full coverage without any third-party server holding your transaction history.

## How to pick

Three honest questions answer it:

**1. Do you pay with Apple Pay more than half the time?**
If yes, Expensa is the only app that captures those taps automatically — no aggregator, no manual entry. The other five all need you to type each transaction or wait for a CSV import.

**2. Do you need to pull data from a US or Canadian bank?**
If yes, Saldo has the broadest bank coverage (700+ institutions). Just know your credentials sit on someone else's server. If you bank with Monobank specifically, Fealthy is the only native sync.

**3. Do you want one number, or fifteen?**
If you want a single monthly target with one progress bar — Expensa starts there and adds category limits only if you ask for them. If you want detailed per-category budgets across the board, Money Manager and FinEye are both built for that.

## What Expensa does that the others don't

Three things show up in Expensa that nothing else in the list above does:

- **Apple Pay sync.** iOS Shortcuts trigger → Expensa action. Every tap-to-pay charge logs itself with merchant, amount, date, and currency, the moment the bank clears it. [Two-minute setup guide here](/blog/how-to-set-up-apple-pay-sync/).
- **AI Smart Budget Allocation.** Most apps let you build a budget. Expensa suggests one — splitting your monthly target across categories based on your real spending history and your country's typical patterns. You override anything you don't like.
- **AI receipt scanning that works on the iOS share sheet.** Snap a photo from anywhere in iOS, share to Expensa, and the amount/merchant/date/currency get extracted automatically. No need to open the app first. Works with invoices, bills, and bank statement PDFs in multiple languages.

None of these are nice-to-haves layered on a tracker — they exist because typing every transaction is why most people abandon every other app on this list.

## The honest trade-offs

**Expensa doesn't aggregate banks.** If your spending is mostly chip-and-pin, online card entry, or banks Apple Pay can't see, you'll be doing CSV imports once a month. That's the price of not handing your bank login to a third party.

**Expensa doesn't track investments.** If you want stocks, ETFs, and crypto in the same app as your coffee receipts, FinEye and Saldo cover that ground today. Expensa is focused on day-to-day spending and budget execution.

**Expensa is iPhone only.** No Android, no web, no Mac native (yet). The other apps in this list mostly span platforms — that matters if your spouse is on Android.

If those trade-offs are dealbreakers, the comparison table above tells you exactly where to go. If they aren't, Expensa is one tap away.
