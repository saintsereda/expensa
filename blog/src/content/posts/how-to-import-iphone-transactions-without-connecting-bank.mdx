Most expense trackers ask for your online banking credentials so a third party (Plaid, Yodlee, MX) can scrape your transactions on your behalf. That works, but you're handing your login to a company you didn't sign up with — and if that pipe ever breaks, your feed goes silent.

There's another way on iPhone. Every Apple Pay tap can log itself to [Expensa](https://apps.apple.com/app/expensa-ai-expenses-manager/id6758392205) automatically — merchant, amount, date — using a one-time iOS Shortcut. No bank password leaves your phone, no third party in the middle, and the transaction lands in your tracker the second the payment clears.

## Why skip the bank connection?

- **No shared credentials.** Aggregators ask for the same username and password you use to log in to your bank. Apple Pay never exposes that — Expensa just receives the merchant name, amount, and date that Wallet already shows you.
- **No third party in the data path.** The Shortcut runs locally on your iPhone. Apple Pay → Shortcuts → Expensa. That's it.
- **Works with any card in Wallet.** Personal debit, business credit, foreign card, virtual card — if it's in Apple Pay, the automation captures it.
- **Doesn't break when your bank changes its login flow.** Aggregator scrapers break constantly. Apple Pay does not.

The trade-off: it only captures purchases you actually pay with Apple Pay. Cash, swiped chip, and online card-number entry don't fire the trigger. For most iPhone users that's still the majority of daily spending — and the rest you can scan or add manually.

## What you'll need

- iPhone with Apple Pay configured
- [Expensa](https://apps.apple.com/app/expensa-ai-expenses-manager/id6758392205) installed
- Shortcuts app (built into iOS — no download needed)

The whole setup takes about 2 minutes.

## Step 1: Open the Shortcuts app

Find the **Shortcuts** app on your iPhone and open it. It comes pre-installed on every iPhone running iOS 13 or later — if you've never opened it, it's probably in the Utilities folder.

![Shortcuts app on iPhone home screen](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-1-shortcuts-app.png)

## Step 2: Go to the Automation tab and tap "New Automation"

At the bottom of the screen, tap the **Automation** tab. Then tap the **New Automation** button to create a new automation.

![Automation tab in Shortcuts](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-2-automation-tab.png)

## Step 3: Select "Wallet"

Scroll down to find **Wallet** — specifically the trigger **"When I tap a Wallet Card or Pass"**. This is the trigger that fires every time you use Apple Pay, and it's why no bank connection is needed: iOS itself tells the Shortcut when a payment happened.

![Selecting the Wallet Transaction trigger](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-3-wallet-trigger.png)

## Step 4: Select your cards and set to Run Immediately

Pick the cards you want the automation to work with — you can include personal, business, and any other card already in Wallet. Then change the run mode to **Run Immediately**. This is important — if it's set to "Run After Confirmation", Shortcuts will ask for your approval every single time you pay, which defeats the whole point. With "Run Immediately" selected, the automation runs silently in the background.

![Disabling Ask Before Running](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-4-ask-before-running.png)

## Step 5: Tap "Create New Shortcut"

When prompted to choose what the automation does, tap **Create New Shortcut**. This opens the action editor where you'll add the Expensa action.

![New Blank Automation screen](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-5-new-blank-automation.png)

## Step 6: Search for and add the Expensa action

In the search bar, type **Expensa** or **Tap to Pay Automation** and select it from the results. This action is what receives the transaction data on Expensa's side — entirely on your device.

![Searching for the Expensa action](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-6-search-action.png)

## Step 7: Map the transaction fields

Now connect the Apple Pay data to the Expensa fields. For each field in the action, tap it → select **Variable** → then **Shortcut Input**, and choose the matching field:

- **Transaction Amount** → Amount
- **Merchant Name** → Merchant Name
- **Transaction Date** → Date *(optional)*

![Mapping transaction fields to variables](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-7-map-fields.png)

![Selecting Shortcut Input for the variable](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-7-1-map-fields.png)

## Step 8: Tap "Done" to save

Tap **Done** in the top-right corner. Your automation is live.

From this point on, every Apple Pay payment will silently log itself to Expensa — amount, merchant, and date — with no action needed from you, and without any bank ever being connected.

![Completed automation](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-8-done.png)

## Choosing which space transactions go to

By default, Apple Pay transactions import into your current active space. You can change this in Expensa under **Settings → Apple Pay Sync** — pick any space you want imported transactions saved to, such as a dedicated "Card" or "Daily" space.

## What about purchases that aren't Apple Pay?

Anything you didn't tap to pay still needs to land in Expensa some other way. Two options that don't require a bank login either:

- **Scan the receipt or bank statement.** Open Expensa → Scan Receipt → camera or photo library. AI extracts the amount, merchant, and date.
- **Import a CSV.** Most banks let you download a statement as CSV from their own app or website. Settings → Import → pick the file. Expensa maps the columns and adds the rows.

Both work fully offline from any aggregator — your bank credentials never leave the bank.

## Troubleshooting

**The automation asks for confirmation every time** — go back into the automation, tap the three-dot menu, and make sure the run mode is set to "Run Immediately".

**Transactions aren't appearing** — check that the Expensa action is the one with all three fields mapped. If any field says "No Variable", tap it and reassign it.

**I can't find the "Tap to Pay Automation" action** — make sure Expensa is installed and open it at least once before searching. iOS needs to register the app's actions in Shortcuts.

**Online card payments aren't being captured** — that's expected. The Wallet trigger only fires for in-person Apple Pay or Apple Pay on the web. For online card-number entry, scan the email receipt or add the transaction manually.
