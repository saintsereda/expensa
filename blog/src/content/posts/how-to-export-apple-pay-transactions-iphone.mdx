Apple Pay handles billions of dollars of transactions a month, and Apple gives you almost no way to get that data out of your phone. Apple Card has a CSV export — but only for Apple Card, and only as a once-a-month statement. Every other card you've added to Wallet — your regular Visa, your Amex, your business card, your debit card — taps through Apple Pay just fine, but the resulting transactions exist nowhere you can download them. Wallet itself doesn't let you export the list it shows you.

If you've ever tried to keep an expense tracker accurate, this is the part where it falls apart. You can see the charge happened. You can see the merchant in Wallet. You just can't get a file out of it.

There's a workaround built into iOS that almost nobody uses — and it captures every Apple Pay tap, on every card, in real time. This post walks through what's actually exportable today, why the gap exists, and how to use Shortcuts to close it.

## What you can actually export from Apple Pay today

A quick honest tally before we get to the workaround.

**Apple Card → CSV: yes, but limited.** From the Wallet app, tap your Apple Card → the "..." menu → Statements → pick a month → Download Transactions (CSV). You get a per-statement CSV with date, amount, merchant, and category. It's the cleanest export Apple ships. Two caveats: it's *only* Apple Card (not the other cards in Wallet), and you only get it once the statement closes — so the most recent ~30 days are unavailable until month-end.

**Every other Apple Pay charge: no export.** A Visa, Amex, or debit card in Wallet runs through Apple Pay the same way Apple Card does, but the transactions Wallet shows for those cards live entirely on your phone, with no file you can download. Apple's official answer is "get the statement from your bank" — which usually means logging into your bank's web app and pulling their CSV, separate from what Wallet shows.

**The merchant-name problem.** Even when you do pull a bank CSV, the merchant names are often a mess: `SQ *MERCHANT NAME LLC`, `PAYPAL*VENDOR`, `AMZN MKTP US*1A2B3C`. Wallet on iPhone is sometimes better — it can show a cleaner merchant name and even a map pin — but sometimes worse, because the name in Wallet is pulled from Apple Maps based on the merchant location, and Apple Maps occasionally tags a transaction with the *next-door* business. A lot of "what is this charge" support threads on Apple's forums are exactly this.

So the realistic picture is: one card gets a delayed monthly CSV, every other card gets nothing, and the merchant names you do see can't always be trusted. That's the problem. Now the workaround.

## The thing iOS can actually do

Since 2021, iOS Shortcuts has had a personal automation trigger called **"When I tap a Wallet Card or Pass"**. It fires every time Apple Pay completes a payment, on any card you select, and it hands the Shortcut three pieces of data:

- The merchant name (as Wallet sees it)
- The transaction amount
- The transaction date

Crucially, this is a real iOS API — not a scraper, not an aggregator. The automation runs locally on your phone. The data goes from Apple Pay straight to whatever Shortcut action you wire it into, with no third party in the path and no bank credentials anywhere.

You can route those three fields into anything Shortcuts supports: append a row to a Google Sheet, add a page to a Notion database, save a note, fire a webhook, or — what most people actually want — send the transaction to an expense tracker that exposes a Shortcuts action.

If you use [Expensa](https://apps.apple.com/app/expensa-ai-expenses-manager/id6758392205), the action is called **"Tap to Pay Automation"** and it accepts those three fields directly. The walkthrough below uses Expensa as the destination; the same pattern works with any app that exposes a Shortcut action for adding a transaction.

A note on merchant names: the Shortcut receives whatever Wallet has at the moment of the tap. That means the cleaner Wallet name (when it's right), and unfortunately also the Apple-Maps-derived name when it's wrong. There's no public iOS API that exposes the raw payment-network merchant string, so no app — including Expensa — can sidestep this. What Expensa does instead is let you rename the merchant once and apply that rename to every future transaction from the same source, which is a one-tap fix the first time it shows up wrong.

## What you'll need

- iPhone with Apple Pay configured
- Shortcuts app (built into iOS — no download needed)
- A destination app for the data — for this walkthrough, [Expensa](https://apps.apple.com/app/expensa-ai-expenses-manager/id6758392205) — but any Shortcuts-aware tracker works

The setup is a one-time thing and takes about two minutes.

## Step 1: Open the Shortcuts app

Find the **Shortcuts** app on your iPhone and open it. It comes pre-installed on every iPhone running iOS 13 or later — if you've never opened it, it's probably in the Utilities folder.

![Shortcuts app on iPhone home screen](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-1-shortcuts-app.png)

## Step 2: Go to the Automation tab and tap "New Automation"

At the bottom of the screen, tap the **Automation** tab. Then tap the **New Automation** button to create a new automation.

![Automation tab in Shortcuts](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-2-automation-tab.png)

## Step 3: Select "Wallet"

Scroll down to find **Wallet** — specifically the trigger **"When I tap a Wallet Card or Pass"**. This is the trigger that fires every time you use Apple Pay, and it's why no bank connection is needed: iOS itself tells the Shortcut when a payment happened.

![Selecting the Wallet Transaction trigger](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-3-wallet-trigger.png)

## Step 4: Select your cards and set to Run Immediately

Pick the cards you want the automation to work with — you can include personal, business, and any other card already in Wallet. Then change the run mode to **Run Immediately**. This is important — if it's set to "Run After Confirmation", Shortcuts will ask for your approval every single time you pay, which defeats the whole point. With "Run Immediately" selected, the automation runs silently in the background.

![Disabling Ask Before Running](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-4-ask-before-running.png)

## Step 5: Tap "Create New Shortcut"

When prompted to choose what the automation does, tap **Create New Shortcut**. This opens the action editor where you'll add the destination action.

![New Blank Automation screen](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-5-new-blank-automation.png)

## Step 6: Add the action that receives the transaction

In the search bar, type the name of your destination app. For Expensa, search **Expensa** or **Tap to Pay Automation** and select it from the results. This action is what receives the transaction data — entirely on your device.

If you want to send the data somewhere else, search for the relevant app: "Append Row" for Google Sheets, "Add to Database" for Notion, "Get Contents of URL" for a webhook, and so on. The next step (mapping fields) is the same regardless of destination.

![Searching for the destination action](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-6-search-action.png)

## Step 7: Map the transaction fields

Now connect the Apple Pay data to the destination fields. For each field in the action, tap it → select **Variable** → then **Shortcut Input**, and choose the matching field:

- **Transaction Amount** → Amount
- **Merchant Name** → Merchant Name
- **Transaction Date** → Date *(optional)*

![Mapping transaction fields to variables](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-7-map-fields.png)

![Selecting Shortcut Input for the variable](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-7-1-map-fields.png)

## Step 8: Tap "Done" to save

Tap **Done** in the top-right corner. Your automation is live.

From this point on, every Apple Pay payment will silently log itself to your tracker — amount, merchant, and date — with no action needed from you, on every card you selected.

![Completed automation](/blog/images/apple-pay-setup/step-8-done.png)

## Choosing which space transactions go to

In Expensa, by default Apple Pay transactions land in your current active space. You can change this under **Settings → Apple Pay Sync** — pick any space you want imported transactions saved to, such as a dedicated "Card" or "Daily" space, or a shared space if you're tracking household spending with someone else.

## What about purchases that aren't Apple Pay?

The Wallet trigger only fires when iOS is the one completing the payment — physical tap, Apple Pay on the web (in Safari), and Wallet-driven Apple Pay in apps. It doesn't fire for:

- Card-number entry on a website
- Chip insert / swipe at a terminal that doesn't take contactless
- Cash
- Auto-renewed subscriptions billed directly by the merchant (not through Apple ID)

For those, two options that still don't require a bank login:

- **Scan a receipt or statement.** Open Expensa → Scan Receipt → camera or photo library. AI extracts the amount, merchant, and date from paper receipts, email confirmations, or a full bank statement PDF.
- **Import a CSV.** Most banks let you download a statement as CSV from their own app or website. Settings → Import → pick the file. Column mapping handles whatever format your bank uses.

Both paths skip any aggregator — your bank credentials never leave the bank's website.

If you specifically left Mint because you didn't want a third-party aggregator scraping your accounts, this combination — Apple Pay automation + CSV imports + receipt scanning — is the full replacement on iPhone. There's a longer walkthrough of [how to rebuild Mint's whole setup on iPhone without Plaid](/blog/how-to-replace-mint-on-iphone-without-plaid).

## Troubleshooting

**The automation asks for confirmation every time** — go back into the automation, tap the three-dot menu, and make sure the run mode is set to "Run Immediately".

**Transactions aren't appearing** — check that the destination action has all three fields mapped. If any field says "No Variable", tap it and reassign it.

**I can't find the "Tap to Pay Automation" action** — make sure Expensa is installed and open it at least once before searching. iOS needs to register the app's actions in Shortcuts.

**Online card payments aren't being captured** — that's expected. The Wallet trigger only fires for in-person Apple Pay or Apple Pay on the web. For online card-number entry, scan the email receipt or add the transaction manually.

**A merchant name is wrong or generic** — this is the Apple-Maps-derived name from Wallet showing up. Rename it once in Expensa and the rename applies to every future transaction from the same source.

**I want Apple Card transactions in the same place too** — Apple Card itself uses Apple Pay, so the Wallet trigger captures Apple Card taps just like any other card. For online Apple Card purchases that didn't use Apple Pay, pull the monthly CSV from Wallet → Apple Card → Statements and import it.
