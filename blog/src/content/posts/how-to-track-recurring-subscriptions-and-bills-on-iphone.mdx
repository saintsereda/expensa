Most people underestimate their subscriptions by half. Three streaming services, two cloud storage plans, a gym you stopped going to in February, a forgotten $4.99 app from 2023 — added up, the average iPhone user is bleeding $80–$200 a month on auto-renewals they can't even name.

The fix isn't another spreadsheet. It's setting up your recurring expenses *once* in [Expensa](https://apps.apple.com/app/expensa-ai-expenses-manager/id6758392205) so every charge logs itself, every month, with a notification before it hits your card.

## What "recurring transactions" actually means in Expensa

A recurring transaction is a template that auto-creates a real transaction on a schedule. You set it up once — Netflix, $15.99, monthly — and Expensa generates the entry on the right day, every time, with a reminder if you want one.

It works for both directions:

- **Expenses:** subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, iCloud+), bills (rent, utilities, insurance), memberships (gym, ChatGPT Plus, that newsletter you forgot)
- **Income:** salary, freelance retainers, dividends, recurring transfers in

You pick the cadence — **daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly** — and Expensa takes it from there.

## How to set up a recurring transaction

The whole flow takes under a minute per item. Here's the process for adding your Netflix subscription.

### Step 1: Open the Recurring tab

From the dashboard, tap the **Recurring** section. This is the home for every recurring template — past, present, and paused.

![Recurring tab in Expensa](/blog/images/recurring/step-1-recurring-tab.png)

### Step 2: Tap the "+" button

The plus button in the top-right opens the new recurrence form.

![New recurrence form](/blog/images/recurring/step-2-new-form.png)

### Step 3: Choose Expense or Income

Pick **Expense** for subscriptions and bills, **Income** for salary and recurring earnings. The form adapts: expense flows into a category like "Subscriptions"; income flows into "Salary" or another income category you define.

![Choosing expense vs income](/blog/images/recurring/step-3-type.png)

### Step 4: Fill in the details

- **Amount and currency** — e.g., 15.99 USD
- **Category** — Subscriptions (or create a new one)
- **Account** — which card or wallet pays it
- **Merchant name** — "Netflix" (this is the label that shows up on every generated transaction)
- **Notes** — optional; e.g., "Family plan, shared with sister"

![Filling in transaction details](/blog/images/recurring/step-4-details.png)

### Step 5: Set the frequency

Pick from **Daily, Weekly, Monthly,** or **Yearly**. For Netflix, that's monthly. For rent, monthly. For an annual iCloud+ plan, yearly.

![Choosing frequency](/blog/images/recurring/step-5-frequency.png)

### Step 6: Set the start date

Pick the next date the charge hits. Expensa uses this as the anchor — every future transaction generates relative to this date. If your Netflix renews on the 14th of every month, set the 14th.

![Setting start date](/blog/images/recurring/step-6-start-date.png)

### Step 7: Turn on notifications (recommended)

Toggle **Notify me** and pick the timing:

- **Day of due date** — wakes you up the morning of the charge
- **Day before due date** — gives you 24 hours to cancel if you don't want to renew

Day-before is the one that catches forgotten subscriptions. Worth it.

![Notification settings](/blog/images/recurring/step-7-notifications.png)

### Step 8: Save

Tap **Save**. From now on, Expensa generates the transaction automatically on every due date, and the notification fires when you set it to.

## A 10-minute audit that will save you money

Here's the workflow that actually exposes how much you're spending on autopilot:

1. Open your bank statement from the last two months
2. For every recurring charge — even the $1.99 ones — add a recurring transaction in Expensa
3. Set notifications to **day-before** for everything
4. Open the Dashboard

You'll see a single "Subscriptions" category with the real number. Most people are shocked. The next month, when the day-before notifications start firing, you'll cancel two or three of them before the charge hits.

That's the entire point.

## Pro tips

**Use Pause instead of Cancel for seasonal stuff.** Going on a 3-month sabbatical and pausing your gym? Pause the recurring transaction. The template stays, generation stops, and you resume it with one tap when you come back. Cancel deletes it.

**Separate income recurrences let you forecast cash flow.** Once your salary is recurring and your fixed expenses are recurring, the dashboard math becomes real: income minus fixed costs equals the actual budget you have left to spend. No more month-end surprises.

**Custom categories beat generic ones.** Don't use "Bills" for everything. Make separate categories for "Streaming," "Software," "Memberships." When you can see *which* subcategory is bloating, you cancel the right thing.

**Multi-currency works out of the box.** Got a Notion subscription billed in USD but you live in Europe? Set it in USD. Expensa stores the original amount + the exchange rate at generation time, so your reports stay accurate even when rates move.

## Troubleshooting

**Transactions aren't generating** — open the app at least once. Recurrence catch-up runs on app launch and during background refresh. If you've been offline for a week, opening the app will create the missed transactions in one shot (no duplicates).

**The notification didn't fire** — check Settings → Notifications and confirm permissions are granted. If iOS denied notifications, none of the day-of or day-before reminders will work.

**I want to change the amount permanently** — open the recurring template (not an individual transaction) and edit the amount there. Future generations will use the new value. Past transactions stay at the amount they were created with.

**The charge happened on a different date** — edit the start date on the template; future transactions will anchor to the new date.
